# Peptides in Skincare: What Works

Peptides are everywhere in anti-aging skincare. But with hundreds of peptides on the market, how do you know which ones actually work?

What Are Peptides?

Peptides are short chains of amino acids‚Äîthe building blocks of proteins like collagen and elastin. In skincare, they act as signaling molecules, telling your skin to produce more of these structural proteins.

Think of them as text messages to your skin cells: "Hey, make more collagen!"

The 5 Categories of Peptides

1. Signal Peptides

What they do: Stimulate fibroblasts to produce collagen, elastin, and other matrix proteins.

Key players:

  • Palmitoyl Pentapeptide-4 (Matrixyl)
  • Palmitoyl Tripeptide-1

2. Carrier Peptides

What they do: Deliver trace elements (like copper) to the skin to aid wound healing and enzyme function.

Key players:

  • Copper Tripeptide-1 (GHK-Cu)

Evidence strength: Strong ‚úÖ

3. Neurotransmitter-Inhibiting Peptides

What they do: Reduce muscle contractions‚Äîlike a topical Botox (but much milder).

  • Acetyl Hexapeptide-3 (Argireline)
  • Pentapeptide-18 (Leuphasyl)

4. Enzyme-Inhibiting Peptides

5. Structural Peptides

## Which Peptides Actually Work?

Based on clinical evidence, here's my honest assessment:

Peptide Evidence My Verdict
Matrixyl 23+ studies Worth using
GHK-Cu 70+ studies Highly effective
Argireline Mixed results Manage expectations
Most others Limited Marketing-driven

The GHK-Cu Story

Copper peptide (GHK-Cu) is the most researched peptide in skincare with over 70 published studies. It's been shown to:

  • Increase collagen production by 70%
  • Improve skin elasticity by 40%
  • Accelerate wound healing
  • Reduce inflammation

The catch? It's expensive and challenging to formulate. Look for products from reputable brands with stable formulations.

Realistic Expectations

Let's be clear: peptides are not magic. They won't:

  • Replace Botox or fillers
  • Reverse 20 years of aging overnight
  • Work if used inconsistently

They will:

  • Provide modest improvement over 2-3 months
  • Support overall skin health
  • Complement other anti-aging strategies

How to Use Peptides

Best Practices:

  1. Consistency is key ‚Äî Use twice daily for best results
  2. Don't combine with low-pH products ‚Äî Acids can denature peptides
  3. Layer properly ‚Äî Apply after water-based serums, before oils/creams
  4. Give it time ‚Äî Expect 8-12 weeks for visible results
